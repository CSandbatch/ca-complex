# Research Notes

This front-end pass was pushed toward a more defensible visual language using a small set of concrete references rather than generic "serious SaaS" patterns.

## What Mattered

### Geist

The useful signal from Geist is not just "nice sans serif." Vercel explicitly frames it around simplicity, minimalism, speed, Swiss design inspiration, precision, clarity, and functionality. That is the right baseline for a product that wants to feel like an instrument rather than a feed.

### Geist Pixel

The useful signal from Geist Pixel is that pixel logic can still ship in modern products when used with system discipline. The important lesson is not to make the whole product retro. The lesson is to use a controlled, grid-based technical texture in labels, rails, and occasional contrast moments.

### Bloomberg

Bloomberg's own framing is useful because it is operational, not decorative: fast, smart, rich data across sectors and workflows, one unified system, and navigation that helps people make effective decisions quickly. That supports the decision to privilege state, compression, and navigation over ornamental interface behavior.

### Mondwest

Mondwest is useful as a warning and a permission slip at the same time. The permission is that a system can have a slightly strange display layer. The warning is that the strangeness has to stay controlled, precise, and legible. That is why the current pass uses editorial or unusual display typography sparingly rather than across the entire product.

### Wim Crouwel

The relevant lesson is grid logic as a design method, not as a visual effect. Crouwel is the right reference for systems that feel organized, technical, and clear without collapsing into blandness. This informed the stronger column logic, alignment, and the refusal of bubbly UI treatment.

## Translation Into This Repo

- The shell uses a restrained neutral field so the active concept can carry the strong identity.
- `Signal Atlas` uses blue-system geometry and an electric yellow reserved for live pressure.
- `Brass Ledger` leans editorial: paper tones, column structure, serif hierarchy, less card dependence.
- `Quiet Ministry` reduces chrome and lets compression do the work.
- `Vector Chamber` shifts toward forensic evidence surfaces rather than another dashboard.
- `Oxide Archive` frames the product as institutional memory instead of analytics software.

## What To Keep Doing

- Use accent colors sparingly and structurally.
- Let typography and alignment produce authority.
- Prefer diagrams, rails, grids, and strips over decorative illustrations.
- Keep one controlled weird note in the system; do not let the entire UI become a gimmick.
